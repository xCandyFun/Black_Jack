Blackjack Rules

Card Values
  • Number cards (2 through 10) are worth their face value.
  • Face cards (Jack, Queen, King) are each worth 10 points.
  • The Ace can be worth either 1 point or 11 points, depending on which is more beneficial for the player.
Game Setup
  • The game is played with a standard deck of 52 cards.
  • The player and the dealer are each dealt two cards at the start of the game.
  • The dealer’s first card is face up, while the second card remains face down.
Player's Turn
  • The player can choose to "Hit" (draw a card) or "Stand" (keep their current hand).
  • The player may continue to draw cards until they either stand or go over 21 points (bust).
  • If the player’s hand exceeds 21 points, they lose immediately.
Dealer's Turn
  • After the player stands, the dealer plays their turn.
  • The dealer must continue to draw cards until their hand value is at least 17 points.
  • If the dealer's hand exceeds 21 points (bust), the player wins.
Winning the Game
  • If the player’s hand value is higher than the dealer's without busting, the player wins.
  • If the dealer’s hand value is higher than the player’s without busting, the dealer wins.
  • If both the player and the dealer have the same hand value, it is a tie (push).
Blackjack
  • If the player or the dealer is dealt an Ace and a 10-point card (10, Jack, Queen, King) as their initial two cards, this is a Blackjack and they win immediately, unless the other player also has a Blackjack, in which case it is a tie.
